Pretraining ✅ done

From-scratch GPT-2-style decoder · PyTorch + tiktoken · trained on WikiText

Headline: a ~30M-parameter decoder built from primitives, trained from scratch on WikiText-103. In one epoch (~115K steps, ~10h on MPS) validation perplexity falls ~1500 → ~100 (val loss ~7.3 → ~4.3); resumed/continued training later pushed it down to ≈ 26–33 — roughly GPT-2-small's range (a model ~4× larger). A strong result at this scale.

Setup

ModelGPT-2-style decoder, built from scratch (~30M params): learned token + positional embeddings → pre-norm Transformer blocks (causal MHA + 4× GELU FFN) → final LayerNorm → LM head (optional weight tying)
Tokenizertiktoken gpt2 BPE (~50K vocab)
DataWikiText-103; documents EOS-joined, sliding-window next-token pairs (split by document, not random window, to avoid val leakage). ~115K steps/epoch at the run's batch size; trained several epochs (checkpoints epoch0–epoch3), some runs resumed from a checkpoint
Objectivenext-token cross-entropy on every position
Success metricvalidation perplexity = exp(val cross-entropy) — the effective number of equally-likely tokens weighed per step (lower = more confident)
Loggingwandb + checkpoints/metrics.csv; qualitative peeks in checkpoints/peek.txt

Qualitative evidence — peek (during → after)

The from-scratch GPT has no instruction behavior to eyeball, so the read is fluency: free-running samples should go from incoherent → repetitive → coherent English as the loss drops. Two fixed peek prompts were generated every 500 steps: "The earth is not flat because" and "King minus male plus female equals".

Step 0 — verbatim floor (random init, loss ≈ 7.3, ppl ≈ 1500)
Peek 1: "The earth is not flat because because because because because …"
Peek 2: "King minus female equals equals equals equals equals …"
Pure single-token repetition — the model has the prompt tokens but no model of what comes next yet.
Mid-epoch-0 — verbatim, ppl ≈ 94.9 (loss 4.55, ~60% through epoch 0, ~8.75h in)
Peek output: "The female 's female 's female 's …" — repetitive, but real English tokens, not random garbage. The classic mid-training signature: the model has learned token statistics and local grammar but collapses into loops. (Captured live from the training console during the fresh run.)
Trained — real samples from checkpoints/peek.txt (resumed/converged checkpoint)
Sample 1: The earth is not flat because the sun is not moving across the sky," the minister said. The US, Australia and Canada are the others who have called for a change in the definition of "flat". But the official said they would be "very careful"

Sample 2: King minus male plus female equals female and the female is considered the mother of the male. For example, a male and female would each be the mother of the male child, but a female and male would each be the mother of the male child. For example

From looping non-sense to grammatical, fluent English with local coherence but semantic drift — exactly the profile expected of a ~30M model (small + data-limited; the distribution is fluent but "mushy", so it wanders over longer spans).

Takeaways

  • Perplexity high → ≈ 30 ≈ GPT-2-small range — competitive with a 4×-larger model, at ~30M params.
  • At small scale you're data-limited — more tokens beat more epochs (extra epochs mostly memorize).
  • The peeks "click" as the curve drops — output goes from incoherent to fluent English; the remaining semantic drift is the expected small-model signature, often a decoding artifact more than a weights one.
