Bradley-Terry RM warmed from the SFT checkpoint · Llama-3.2-1B · Tulu-3 preference mixture · MPS (M5 Pro, 48GB)
Ablation: reward-L2 = 1e-3 vs no regularization (1e-3 → 0)

Two questions, two answers. (1) Did RM training work? Yes — preference accuracy climbs from chance (~0.45) to ≈ 0.68 (near the human-agreement ceiling) and the fixed peeks flip from mis-ranked to all-✓ with widening margins. (2) What does the reward-L2 regularizer do? It leaves ranking quality unchanged but keeps reward magnitudes bounded near 0; without it the rewards drift onto a growing positive pedestal (final r ≈ +0.6 to +1.3) — the unbounded scale that would later swamp PPO's KL penalty. Conclusion: ship l2_reg=1e-3 for the RM that feeds PPO.

Part 1 · Did RM training work?

The question: can a scalar head bolted onto the SFT backbone learn to score a human-preferred answer above a rejected one? The success signals are preference accuracy (fraction of val pairs where r_chosen > r_rejected; chance = 0.5) and the peeks (do fixed pairs flip to ✓ with a widening margin?). Numbers below are the shipped reg = 1e-3 run; the no-reg run reaches the same band (it's the subject of Part 2).

Part 2 · How does the L2 regularizer influence training & outputs?

Part 1 showed both runs rank equally well. So what does the l2_reg term actually change? Its job is not ranking — it's reward scale. The Bradley-Terry loss depends only on the margin (r_chosen − r_rejected), so it leaves the rewards' absolute offset and scale completely free to drift. The L2 penalty λ·mean(r_chosen² + r_rejected²) pulls both rewards toward 0 symmetrically — bounding scale without touching the margin.

Validation mean reward vs training step (the runaway)

reg (solid: r_chosen, faint: r_rejected) stays clamped around 0 · no-reg (dashed) drifts upward off the r=0 line and keeps climbing.

Read: the reg rewards stay pinned near 0 and roughly symmetric (chosen slightly +, rejected slightly −). The no-reg rewards drift onto a growing positive pedestal — by the end both chosen and rejected are large and positive (+1.26 / +0.63), still rising. Nothing in the BT loss constrains that absolute level (shift-invariance), so it wanders freely. The peeks make it even starker: no-reg peek rewards reach +2.6 with margins to +2.5, vs reg peeks around +0.5 to +1.9 — same ranking, ~2–3× inflated scale.

Why this matters for PPO: PPO consumes the reward as r_total = r_RM − β·KL(policy‖ref). An unbounded, drifting r_RM (the no-reg picture) overwhelms the KL term, so the policy ignores the reference and reward-hacks; and a "good" β stops transferring between RMs/runs. The L2-bounded reward keeps that balance sane. (Per-prompt offset is separately absorbed by PPO's value baseline; this L2 term controls global scale.)

2.2 · The margin is preserved despite the scale difference

The margin is preserved despite the scale difference. The two runs end at wildly different reward levels — but the gap they care about is the same:

Same discriminative margin (~0.6–0.7); the only thing that moved is the offset the margin rides on (≈0 vs ≈+0.95). That's the whole point of the L2: it slides the pair back down toward 0 without squeezing them together — ranking untouched, scale tamed. This is shift-invariance made literal.

2.4 · A note on stability

The no-reg val loss bottomed at 0.648 (step 892) then crept back up to 0.674 as the rewards inflated; the reg run descended monotonically to 0.607. This is consistent with the regularizer having a stabilizing effect, but the two runs were not seed-controlled (different random score-head init), so I won't claim L2 strictly lowers BT loss — only that it didn't hurt, and it kept rewards bounded. Reproducing with a fixed seed would settle it.

Takeaways

  • Part 1 — RM training works: accuracy chance → ~0.68, peeks all-✓ with widening margins.
  • 0.66–0.68 is healthy, not weak — near the human-agreement ceiling for a 1B model on a 20K subset, 1 epoch.
  • Part 2 — reward-L2 is free insurance: same accuracy either way, but bounded reward scale only with the penalty.
  • Shift-invariance is real and visible: with no reg, both rewards drift onto a growing positive pedestal while the margin barely changes — the BT loss simply doesn't constrain the absolute level.
  • Decision: ship l2_reg=1e-3 as the RM that feeds PPO.
